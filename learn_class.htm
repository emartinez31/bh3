Bring Your Classroom

BHS offers several content-rich experiences for students from kindergarten to grade 12 with programs that align with state and national learning standards.

An Ancestor’s Trunk

In this program, the youngest members of the community are given the chance to explore an antique trunk filled with authentic artifacts. By examining its contents, students learn about life as it was in the early 1900s in Northeast Queens. They are asked to analyze and compare life as it existed then to life as it is today. Through this program, students are introduced to fundamental research skills such as brainstorming, analysis, and evaluation. The contents of the trunk include vintage photographs of children at play, clothing, toys, household items, school-books, etc. Weather permitting; students are invited outdoors to play with the toys from that era.

Recommended Grades: 2 to 5

Investigative Research

Junior and Senior High School students are invited to explore BHS’s teaching collections and assume the role of “archivist-for-a-day”. They are given historical problems to solve and must use archival material and critical thinking skills to solve them. In doing so, they expand their knowledge of local history and develop their documentary research skills.

Recommended Grades: 6 - 12

Digging Up the Past

This program is dedicated to the study of the Matinecock people, this region’s earliest indigenous settlers who were part of the Algonquin nation. BHS’s permanent exhibit Native Bayside complements the program and provides a rich backdrop for students to learn how artifacts are analyzed, recorded, preserved, and stored. The inclusion of a sandbox archeology workshop using tolls and materials of archeologists provoke students to interactively explore areas of science, anthropology, and sociology.

Recommended Grades: 4 - 5
